    Answers to our frequently
    asked questions

    SpaceHound uses ML models trained on data from thousands of historical LEO missions combined with real-time telemetry, space weather feeds, and subsystem-level sensor data. Our failure prediction engine identifies early anomaly signatures across power, thermal, communication, and propulsion subsystems, often days or weeks before a failure manifests.
    We partner with major launch and insurance firms, making SpaceHound's data-backed certification a trusted standard for launch approval. Our methodology is transparent, auditable, and built on the same mission data standards used by ESA and NASA. Insurers and providers benefit from consistent, independent third-party verification that reduces underwriting uncertainty.
    Existing tools like Space-Track or LeoLabs focus on debris tracking and collision avoidance. SpaceHound goes further: we provide mission-level risk intelligence across the full lifecycle — from design review to on-orbit anomaly response — and deliver that intelligence in formats operators, investors, and insurers can all use directly.
    SpaceHound operates on a SaaS subscription model with tiers for operators, investors, and insurers. We also offer enterprise API access for large-scale integrations and a certification marketplace where operators can receive verified Launch Readiness Certificates. Long-term, we plan to offer actuarial data licensing to the insurance market.
